Our Data

What You Should Know before You Adopt!

Line Graph: This graph shows you a count of the number of dogs in the Austin Pets Alive system by age. We can see that the organization tends to have a large number of younger dogs in their care. It is important for you to consider your lifestyle when choosing a dog to join your family. It is worth noting that younger dogs take time and need constant attention, whereas, older dogs may require increased medical attention but less supervison.

Sunburst Chart: This interactive chart gives a good visual representation of our findings with respect to the dogs in the Austin Pets Alive system. This chart shows an exact break down of how many male and female dogs are in foster care or physically at the APA location as well as how many of these are puppies, considered 6 months or younger.

Honey Comb & Spider Chart: Use these charts to determine which dog is most compatable with your lifestyle. These interactive charts are very interesting to use. Each hexagon represents a dog. The color represents the likeliness each dog has to be adopted based on an average. The average we used comes from the spider chart on the right. These stats come directly from the Austin Pets Alive website and they represent how well each dog does with other dogs, cats, children, and how it does home alone. We took the average of these numbers to determine the color associated with each dog.
